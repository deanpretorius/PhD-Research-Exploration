# Project Brief: PhD Research Exploration

## Core Objective
Dean Pretorius's PhD research at NYU Abu Dhabi's space systems engineering track, supervised by Professor Fares and collaborating with Dr. Hozefa Jizwada (postdoc). The research focuses on advancing robotic locomotion and manipulation through mathematical modeling of friction dynamics using differential geometry and data-driven methods.

## Primary Research Areas

### 1. Grassmannian/Riemannian Manifolds for Friction Modeling
- **Status:** Active research direction
- **Goal:** Extend Dean's prior master's work (friction cone computation in 3D using optimization with maximum dissipation principle) from monopod/biped robots to multi-legged robots (hexapod/octopod)
- **Approach:** Use Grassmannian manifolds to reduce computational complexity of friction optimization across multiple contact points
- **Key Insight:** Friction cones are themselves Riemannian manifolds — this provides the mathematical bridge to Grassmannian methods

### 2. Koopman Operator Theory
- **Status:** Exploratory direction
- **Goal:** Linearize nonlinear dynamics in robotic systems for more tractable analysis and control
- **Connection:** Data-driven Koopman/EDMD methods for analyzing system dynamics
- **Related:** Flow matching paper shared by Dr. Hozefa

### 3. Force Flow Dynamics
- **Status:** Exploratory direction
- **Goal:** Understand and model force propagation through robotic systems during locomotion

### 4. Systems Engineering Integration
- **Goal:** Apply NASA Systems Engineering Handbook as a framework for requirements gathering, use case analysis, and stakeholder management
- **Approach:** Edge-to-cloud computing with virtual reality components for demonstration
